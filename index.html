About Me

Hello! I'm Tanmay, an engineer in the field of robotics. I am passionate about technology in general and hence robotics (a culmination of all). Envisioning the future of robotics and a reality where they truly co-exist with humans has been my question of the year. Physics, large models and business would broadly be the keywords that have excited me in the past few years.

Currently I am pursuing an M.S in Robotics Systems Development at the Robotics Institute (SCS), Carnegie Mellon University. Here I am developing a mobile manipulator for hospital logistics mainly serving the Operating Rooms. I also work with Professor Jean Oh in the Bot Intelligence Group (BIG Lab) where I am trying my hands on self-supervised life-long learning and improvement of policies basically generalist agents.

Before moving to the States I co-founded an agri-tech startup Fambo with a few of my collegues from Addverb. Prior to that I worked as a Robotics engineer for Addverb Technologies, one of the hottest startups in the Indian robotics scene and now a global warehouse robotics player. I can safely say I deployed the first fleet of AMRs for Addverb in the Reliance Jamnagar Refinery. It was a big deal for me then, I learnt a ton here.

During undergrad I was part of RAMAN Lab & ZINE robotics and research group at MNIT Jaipur. I made my first hardware, Software and PCB here. Here I worked under Professor Rajesh Kumar.

Self Supervised Guided goal Discovery via Visual Language Models

In this work, we present a novel approach to self-supervised discovery of skills in robotic systems. Our methodology combines the world knowledge captured by Visual Language Models (VLM) and the exploration capabilities of curiosity-based RL, to autonomously identify and categorize semantically meaningful skills from video data.

Fortress

Nothing special but I made a camera localised robot with obstacle avoidance capabilites with all custom built hardware even a self etched PCB. This was my first robot I made back in 2017.

Used just opencv for all the colour detection and localisation stuff and simple grid with backtracking stuff for path planning (which I later got to know that we implemented something close to A* without knowing it)
