About Me

I'm Tanmay Agarwal a grad student at the Robotics Institute @Carnegie Mellon. Physics, large models and startups would broadly be the keywords that occupy my major mind space.

Currently I am pursuing an M.S in Robotics Systems Development at the Robotics Institute (SCS), Carnegie Mellon University. Here I am developing a mobile manipulator for hospital logistics mainly serving the Operating Rooms for my capstone project. I work with Professor Jean Oh in the Bot Intelligence Group (BIG Lab) with my work focussed on guiding policies using Large Multimodal modals to perform complex tasks without human supervision.

Before this I co-founded an agri-tech startup Fambo with a few of my collegues from Addverb. Prior to that I worked as a Robotics engineer for Addverb Technologies, one of the hottest startups in the Indian robotics scene and now a global warehouse robotics player. I can safely say I deployed the first fleet of AMRs for Addverb in the Reliance Jamnagar Refinery. It was a big deal for me then, I learnt a ton here.

During undergrad I was part of RAMAN Lab & ZINE robotics and research group at MNIT Jaipur. I made my first hardware, Software and PCB here. Here I worked under Professor Rajesh Kumar.

Self Supervised Guided goal Discovery via Visual Language Models

In this work, we present a novel approach to self-supervised discovery of skills in robotic systems. Our methodology combines the world knowledge captured by Visual Language Models (VLM) and the exploration capabilities of curiosity-based RL, to autonomously identify and categorize semantically meaningful skills from video data.
